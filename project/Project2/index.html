Application of Deep Learning in Echo Planar Imaging-based Quantitative Susceptibility Mapping Reconstruction

Purpose

Quantitative susceptibility mapping (QSM) has been shown the promising results in quantifying tissue susceptibility. However, echo-planar imaging (EPI) can provide a fast acquisition as compared with the conventional gradient-recalled-echo sequence. We aimed to evaluate the EPI-based QSM reconstruction using an algorithm of deep learning neural network.

Materials and Methods

QSM data were acquired on a 3T scanner using a 2D gradient echo EPI sequence with: TR/TE = 2500/25 ms, flip angle=90°, bandwidth=250kHz, voxel size=1.8x1.8x1.8mm3. Multi-channel complex data were combined for optimal phase reconstruction and phase data were unwrapped with a Laplacian-based approach. The background field was removed using the projection onto dipole field method1. Susceptibility was obtained by solving the dipole inversion using a recent developed convolutional deep learning network (QSMnet)2. We compared the results of QSMnet with the method of morphology enabled dipole inversion (MEDI)3. Twenty-seven healthy subjects and 26 patients with Parkinson’s disease (PD) were enrolled. A test-retest procedure was performed on the PD cohort to test the reproducibility of QSMnet with two scans separated by one to two weeks.

Results

Figure 1 demonstrated a strong correlation of the calculated susceptibility between QSMnet and MEDI. However, QSMnet significantly reduced the streaking artifacts in EPI-based susceptibility mapping as compared with MEDI (Figure 2).

QSMnet further showed a narrower 95% limits of agreement compared to MEDI in the Bland-Altman analysis of reproducibility (Figure 3). Figure 4 shows the results of calculated susceptibility for each subgroup obtained using the QSMnet and MEDI methods. MEDI generated higher estimated susceptibility than QSMnet in both cohorts. We did not find any significant group difference of susceptibility between two cohorts.

Conclusion

Deep learning showed a promising result with increased accuracy by reducing the streaking artifact in EPI-based QSM as compared with MEDI. Deep learning method also revealed a higher reproducibility as compared with MEDI.
